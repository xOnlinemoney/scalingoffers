6-Day Pre-Webinar Instagram Story Blueprint

Build anticipation, stack credibility, disrupt beliefs, and drive registrations for the LIVE class.

The Core Positioning

The language is:

  • •"Having me in your corner"
  • •"What happens when I'm locked in with you for 30 days"
  • •"Something I've never offered before"
  • •"I'm opening the door for a few people"
  • •"I'm about to change the game for a small group"

Every story should make people feel like they're about to get access to a cheat code that only a few people will ever get.

Day 1 — The Signal

Psychological Job: "Wait... what is he about to do?"

No link. Just intrigue.

The goal is NOT to announce anything. It's to create a disturbance. Something feels different. His audience should feel like something big is coming without being told what.

Story 1: The Cold Open (Lifestyle Video)

Visual: Him in a high-end Vegas setting. Penthouse, casino VIP area, courtside. Living. Not posing.

Text overlay: "What if you had me in your corner for 30 days straight?"

No link. No CTA. No explanation. Just the question hanging in the air.

Story 2: The Tease (Talking Head, 15s max)

"I've never done this before. And I might not do it again. Details coming this week. Pay attention."

Poll Sticker "Would you want me breaking down games with you LIVE?" YES / I'M SLEEP
Why the poll: Everyone who taps YES just raised their hand. That's your DM list for Day 6.
Story 3: The Quotable (Text-only, black bg, white text)

"Most people bet alone. The ones who win have someone in the room who sees what they don't."

Share Arrow Prompt "Send this to someone who needs to hear it."
Why: Shareable, screenshottable, and it frames the entire offer without revealing it. "Someone in the room" is the seed for live game analysis without ever saying it.

Day 2 — The Track Record

Psychological Job: "This man doesn't miss."

Pure credibility. No selling.

Day 2 is pure credibility. No selling. No teasing. Just an avalanche of proof that Mazi is one of the best in the world at this. CEMENT it so hard that when the offer drops, the only question is "how do I get in" not "is he legit."

Story 1: The Receipts (Screenshot Series)

3-4 story frames. Each one a different bet slip, payout, or win. Recent ones. Big numbers.

"Tuesday."
"Last week."
"This one was fun."
"Regular day."
Why: No bragging. No explanation. Just results with timestamps. The casual tone is the flex. When you treat $10K wins like it's a regular Tuesday, that says more than any sales pitch.
Story 2: The Perspective (Talking Head)

"People always ask me, 'Mazi, how do you see games that other people don't?' It's not magic. I just do this every single day. While other people are guessing, I'm studying. While they're chasing parlays, I'm reading the game in real time. I see things before they happen because I've trained my eye for years. This is my life. Not a side hustle. Not a hobby. My life. And this week I'm about to show a few people what that looks like up close."

Why: Transitions from proof to intrigue. "Up close" teases the offer without naming it. "A few people" creates scarcity before anything is announced. "I see things before they happen" plants the seed for live game analysis.
Story 3: The Social Proof (Repost or Screenshot)

Screenshot a comment, DM, or post from someone credible acknowledging his track record. Another bettor, a media mention, a celebrity interaction.

Text: "They know."

No CTA. No link. Just proof stacking.

Story 4: The Seed

Text overlay on lifestyle clip:

"Imagine being in the room while I break down a game live. Hearing exactly what I see before the lines move. That's all I'll say for now."

Why: "In the room" and "break down a game live" is the clearest preview of the offer without naming it. "Before the lines move" speaks directly to bettors who understand value and timing.

Day 3 — The Disruption

Psychological Job: "Everything I've been doing is wrong."

Day 3 is where Mazi separates himself from every capper, tout, and betting page on Instagram. He's not one of them. He's above them. The audience needs to feel that gap so the offer feels like an elevation, not a purchase.

Story 1: The Hot Take (Talking Head)

"You know what's crazy? 90% of people who bet on sports are just donating money to the sportsbook. And they don't even know it. They think they're gambling. They're not. They're just paying. Following random cappers. Tailing parlays on Twitter. Putting money on their favorite team because 'they got a feeling.' That's not betting. That's hoping. And hope don't pay rent."

Why: This is the kind of content that gets screenshotted and sent to group chats. It attacks the behavior without insulting people directly. Makes them feel seen. Positions Mazi as the guy who does it differently.
Story 2: The Live Analysis Tease (Video)

Show him watching a game. Phone or screen visible. Reacting, pointing something out, calling something before it happens. Raw footage. Like someone accidentally recorded him in his element.

"This is what most bettors will never see. What it looks like when someone's actually reading the game in real time."

Why: At $25K, you're selling the mystique as much as the results. Showing him in the middle of live analysis without explaining the process makes people want IN. They see the skill but can't access it. The only way to get it is through the offer.
Story 3: The Contrast (Text-only, two frames)

Frame 1 (dark)

What most people do: Pick a team. Bet. Pray. Lose. Repeat.

Frame 2 (gold)

What I do: Watch. Read. Analyze. Position. Win. Repeat.

Simple. Visual contrast. No CTA needed. The contrast does the selling.

Story 4: The Whisper

"Tomorrow I'm going to tell you what I'm doing. If you've been paying attention this week, you already know it's different."

Why: Building to the "announcement" on Day 4 without actually announcing. He's been teasing for 3 days. The anticipation is the marketing.

Day 4 — The Reveal (But Not Really)

Psychological Job: "I need to be on this. Whatever it is."

Links start today

Day 4 is when Mazi "reveals" what he's doing. But the reveal is intentionally vague. He tells them WHAT (a live class) but not the specifics, the price, or the details. The webinar is the reveal. Today just confirms something is happening and gives them a way in.

Story 1: The Announcement (Talking Head — Most Important Story of the Week)

"Alright. I've been hinting at this all week. Here it is. I'm going live [DATE] to break down something I've never offered before. I'm not gonna tell you exactly what it is yet. But I will tell you this: if you've ever wanted me in your corner, actually in your corner, breaking down live games in real time, showing you exactly what I see before the lines move, calling out plays as they happen... this is the closest you're ever gonna get. It's not for everybody. It's for a few people who are serious. Register for the live. I'll explain everything there."

Why: "Never offered before" = novelty. "Not gonna tell you exactly" = mystery. "Breaking down live games in real time" = picture without naming. "Not for everybody" = pre-qualifies. "A few people who are serious" = scarcity + identity. Only way to find out more = register.
Story 2: The Backup (Text)

"Next [DAY], I'm going live to explain something that's going to change how a small group of people makes money from sports. That's all I can say right now. Link to register below."

Link Sticker DM me "LOCKED" for the link
Why "LOCKED" not "SYSTEM": The word "locked" fits his persona. It implies exclusivity. "I'm locked in." "We're locked." Natural to his audience.
Story 3: The Scarcity Frame

"I'm not doing this for clout. I'm not doing this to sell you a course. I'm doing this because a few people have been asking for something I've never given access to. If that's you, register. If not, no love lost."

Why: Handles the "is this a scam/course/guru thing" objection before it forms. "Not for clout" and "not a course" disarms skepticism. "A few people have been asking" = demand-driven, not sales-driven.
Story 4: Engagement Bait
Poll Sticker "You coming to the live?" I'M THERE / TELL ME MORE

Anyone who taps either option gets a DM.

Day 5 — Pain + Desire

Psychological Job: Feel the cost of missing this.

SHORT DAY — Only 3 stories

Only 3 stories today. Intentionally light. After 4 days of content, pull back. Less noise = more weight per frame.

Story 1: The Pain (Text, dark background, slow reveal)

"Another month goes by."

"Same bets. Same losses. Same cycle."

"You know something has to change."

"But you keep doing it alone."

No sticker. No link. No CTA. Just the weight.

Why: At $25K, the pain isn't "I can't afford food." The pain is: "I keep losing and I know I shouldn't be. I know there's a better way and I'm not accessing it." That's a person who HAS money to bet but doesn't have the edge. That's Mazi's buyer.
Story 2: The Desire (Lifestyle + Talking Head)

Visual: Him watching a game, relaxed, locked in. Calm confidence. Maybe a phone notification of a win hits mid-clip.

"This is what it looks like when you're not guessing. When you're watching games with someone who sees things nobody else sees. Breaking down plays live. Reading the game before it happens. That's what I'm offering. The live explains everything."

Why: "Someone who sees things nobody else sees" is the most powerful positioning for live game analysis. It makes Mazi feel like a superpower you can borrow.
Story 3: The Bridge

"Tomorrow I'm showing you what people who've been around me already know. These results aren't normal. They're earned. And for the first time, I'm letting people inside."

Link Sticker DM "LOCKED"
Why the teaser for tomorrow: You're pre-selling Day 6's social proof. They'll come back to check.

Day 6 — Proof + Final Push

Psychological Job: "I can't afford to miss this."

Heaviest Day — 7+ stories. This is the close.
Morning
10am–12pm
Stories 1–3: Social Proof Stack

Client wins. DMs from people who've been around him during games. Screenshots of reactions. Before/after lifestyle. Stack 2-3 proof frames.

"He was in the room when I called it live."
"Real time. No delay. I said it before it happened."
"This is what it sounds like when I'm locked in during a game."

Keep it vague. Never say "this is a client." Never name the product. Just show the results. The implication is clear: people close to Mazi during games win.

Story 4: The Mirror

"Every person I've helped started in the same place. Losing more than winning. Betting with emotion. No edge. The only difference between them and you is they got in the room."

Countdown Sticker
Afternoon
3pm–5pm
Story 5: FAQ Rapid Fire (Text frames, one per story)
"Do I need a big bankroll?" No.
"Is this for beginners?" If you watch sports and bet, you qualify.
"Is this a picks service?" Way more. I'll explain on the live.
"Will there be a replay?" No. Live only. One time.
"Why should I trust you?" Show up. Judge for yourself.
Why "way more than that" on picks: You can't say it's just picks at $25K. "Way more. I'll explain on the live" keeps the mystery and forces registration.
Evening
2–3 hours before
Story 6: The Final Call (Talking Head)

"We're going live tonight. I'm about to put something on the table I've never offered before. If you've been watching me all week, you already feel it. This is different. Register right now. Link is in the story and the bio. Once we start, the door closes."

Countdown Sticker Link Sticker DM "LOCKED" right now
Story 7: Behind the Scenes

Photo/video of his setup. Screens. Game feeds. The war room.

"Setting up. Tonight changes the game for somebody watching this right now."

Story 8: 15-Minute Warning

"15 MINUTES. If you're not registered, you're missing it. Link in bio."

Clean. No stickers. Just urgency.

Daily Rules — Every Day, No Exceptions

  • 3–5 story frames daily (Day 6 gets 7+)
  • At least 1 talking-head video every day
  • Link sticker on minimum 2 stories (starting Day 4)
  • "DM 'LOCKED'" on at least 1 story per day (starting Day 4)
  • Days 1–3 have NO registration link. Just intrigue. Links appear Day 4.
  • Mix formats: video, text-only, screenshots, lifestyle, interactive stickers
  • Film raw on phone. No production. No editing. Overproduced content kills trust.
  • Post between 11am–1pm and 7pm–10pm
