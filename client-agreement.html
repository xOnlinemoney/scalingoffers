WEBINAR PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

TRIAL PERIOD & REVENUE-SHARING AGREEMENT

TRUE CAPITAL HOLDINGS, LLC d/b/a SCALING OFFERS

EFFECTIVE DATE:

This Webinar Partnership Agreement ("This Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date above, by and between:

True Capital Holdings, LLC, a Florida limited liability company doing business as Scaling Offers, with its principal place of business in Miami, Florida (hereinafter referred to as the "Company"); and

The undersigned individual or business entity as identified in the signature section below (hereinafter referred to as the "Partner" or "Offer Owner").

The Company and the Partner may each be referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

RECITALS

WHEREAS, the Company operates a business that facilitates webinars, sales presentations, client enrollment, and client funding services under the brand name Scaling Offers;

WHEREAS, the Partner possesses a high-ticket coaching, consulting, training, or service offer that the Partner desires to sell through Company-facilitated webinars;

WHEREAS, the Company provides financing access, sales infrastructure, webinar hosting, enrollment support, and marketing assistance to enable the Partner to generate revenue;

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to set forth the terms and conditions of their business relationship, including a trial period, profit-sharing structure, compliance obligations, and mutual responsibilities;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and promises contained herein, and for other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

1. Purpose

This Agreement outlines the business relationship between the Company and the Partner. The Partner will collaborate with the Company to:

  • Present webinars using the Company's systems, scripts, and processes
  • Promote and sell the Partner's coaching, consulting, or training services
  • Assist with the enrollment process for prospective clients
  • Generate approved client sales using Company-approved financing partners

This Agreement applies only after the Partner successfully completes the required Trial Webinar as defined in Section 2.

2. Trial Period — One (1) Webinar Requirement

2.1 Trial Webinar. Before this Agreement becomes fully active, the Partner must complete one (1) full live webinar using the Company's scripts, systems, and processes (the "Trial Webinar").

2.2 Evaluation. The Company will evaluate the Partner's performance during the Trial Webinar based on, but not limited to: professionalism, communication, collaboration, adherence to the Company's sales process, and overall presentation quality.

2.3 Activation. This Agreement becomes fully active only upon the Company's written approval following the successful completion of the Trial Webinar. The Company has sole and absolute discretion in determining whether the Trial Webinar meets its standards.

2.4 Failure to Meet Standards. If the Partner fails to meet the Company's standards or fails to complete the Trial Webinar, the Company may terminate this relationship immediately with no further obligation to the Partner.

3. Term of Agreement

3.1 Initial Term. Upon successful completion of the Trial Webinar and Company approval, this Agreement shall remain active for a period of twelve (12) months from the date of approval (the "Initial Term").

3.2 Renewal. This Agreement may be renewed thereafter by mutual written agreement of both Parties.

3.3 Partner-Initiated Termination. The Partner may request termination of this Agreement, but only after: (a) all clients brought through Company-approved financing have completed their financing payments in full; or (b) a verified completion date for final repayment has been provided and confirmed by the Company. The Company may approve early termination once all lender repayment responsibilities are satisfied.

3.4 Company-Initiated Termination. The Company may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if: (a) the Partner materially breaches any provision of this Agreement; (b) the Partner engages in unethical conduct, fraud, dishonesty, or willful misconduct; (c) confidential information is leaked or disclosed in violation of this Agreement; (d) the Partner fails to perform their duties as outlined herein; (e) negative client fulfillment issues arise that are attributable to the Partner; (f) the Partner's conduct or client performance jeopardizes the Company's lender relationships; or (g) the Company fails to provide leads, sales support, or team assistance necessary for generating revenue.

3.5 Pending Commissions. Upon termination by either Party, any pending commissions for valid, completed, and funded sales shall still be paid to the Partner in accordance with Section 4.

4. Profit-Sharing Structure

4.1 Revenue Split. All approved revenue generated through Company-facilitated webinars and enrollment shall be split as follows:

  • 50% to the Partner
  • 50% to the Company

4.2 Mandatory Deductions Before Split. Before the 50/50 profit split is calculated, the following mandatory deductions shall be applied to gross revenue:

  • 2% — Enrollment Specialist Fee
  • 6% — Financing Company Funding Fee

4.3 Advertising Deductions. Any verified advertising spend used to promote the Partner's webinar or offer—whether managed through the Company's in-house media buying team or other approved channels—shall be deducted from gross revenue (top-line) prior to any profit-share calculations. This ensures transparency and accurate expectations for both Parties.

4.4 Media Buying and Advertising. The Company shall provide an in-house media buyer and cover all advertising costs on behalf of the Partner. All advertising spend shall be recouped by the Company from gross revenue (top-line) prior to any profit-share calculations. No minimum revenue or capital threshold is required for the Partner to receive media buying support.

5. Payout Terms and Financing

5.1 Offer Range. This profit-sharing structure applies to Partner offers priced between Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00) and Twenty-Five Thousand Dollars ($25,000.00). Ninety percent (90%) of Partners choose a Twenty-Five Thousand Dollar ($25,000.00) offer price due to the high in-person training value.

5.2 Alternative Financing. In cases where applicants do not meet traditional credit requirements, an alternative private-equity-backed loan option may be utilized. This pathway is designed to support applicants with weaker credit by assessing eligibility based on verified income, bank activity, and documentation rather than credit score alone.

5.3 Credit Denial. A client may be fully denied financing due to factors including, but not limited to: collections, charge-offs, recent bankruptcy, high debt-to-income ratio, or income verification issues. The Company is not liable for client denials.

5.4 Commission Disbursement. Commissions are paid only on approved and closed sales. Once the Company receives funds from the financing partner, Partner payouts shall occur within two (2) business days. The Company shall provide the Partner with financing reports and sales tracking sheets.

5.5 Payment Methods. Clients may pay with debit or credit card directly to the Partner, or use Company-approved financing (highly recommended). Financing is recommended because clients retain their cash for business growth, low monthly payments increase conversions, and historically approximately eighty percent (80%) of clients choose financing.

6. Chargebacks, Refunds, and Financing Policies

6.1 Chargebacks (Debit/Credit Card Payments). If a client files a chargeback on a debit or credit card payment: (a) the Company must issue a refund if the payment processor rules in the client's favor; (b) Partner commissions for that sale shall be reversed; and (c) if commissions were already paid, they shall be deducted from future payouts.

6.2 Financing Clients — No Chargebacks / No Recourse. The Company's financing partners do not allow chargebacks, do not pull back funds, and operate on no-recourse terms. However, if the Company observes that a Partner's clients are not making payments, not fulfilling financing obligations, or are not remaining satisfied, the Company may terminate the partnership to protect its premium lender relationships.

7. Compliance and Lender Relationship Protection

7.1 Client Financing Compliance. The Partner must ensure that all clients enrolled through Company-approved financing partners: (a) remain in full compliance with the terms of their financing agreement; (b) make all required payments on time; and (c) complete payment for both the financing obligation and the Partner's program.

7.2 Monthly Compliance Verification. The Company will provide the Partner with a monthly compliance verification form. The Partner is required to: (a) send this compliance form to each financed client every month; (b) collect proof of on-time payment from the financing company or lender; and (c) confirm the client is actively completing payments for the Partner's program.

7.3 Submission Requirement. The Partner must submit completed monthly compliance forms and payment proof to the Company within the required reporting timeframe each month.

7.4 Mandatory Condition. Monthly compliance verification is mandatory and is a condition of maintaining the partnership. Failure to: (a) send compliance forms to clients; (b) collect proof of on-time payment; (c) submit required documentation; or (d) ensure client compliance with lender terms may result in: suspension of payouts, immediate termination of the partnership, and/or removal of access to financing partners.

7.5 Lender Relationship Protection. The Company reserves the right to immediately terminate this Agreement if the Partner's clients demonstrate repeated non-compliance, late payments, or behavior that jeopardizes the Company's lender relationships. Protection of lender relationships is a top priority and supersedes all other partnership considerations.

8. Company Responsibilities

The Company agrees to:

  • Assist with webinar lead generation through paid advertising and/or organic marketing strategies, sales support, and operational assistance
  • Supply scripts, marketing assets, and proven systems
  • Maintain communication and performance coaching
  • Process payouts accurately and in a timely manner
  • Support the Partner in maximizing conversions
  • Protect lender relationships
  • Provide updates on loan origination dates and payout timelines
  • Deliver reports for financing and sales tracking

9. Partner Responsibilities

The Partner agrees to:

  • Promote and sell their coaching, consulting, or training services through Company-facilitated webinars
  • Follow the Company's standards, systems, and procedures at all times
  • Collaborate with the Company's Enrollment Specialists
  • Maintain professionalism and strong communication throughout the partnership
  • Provide performance updates or reports as requested by the Company
  • Maintain confidentiality of all Company information
  • Deliver their offer to clients and ensure client success and satisfaction
  • Handle all fulfillment and client results related to the Partner's program
  • Maintain a high-ticket offer priced between $10,000 and $25,000

If the Partner does not have a proven high-ticket offer, the Company's webinar hosts will guide them in restructuring their offer to meet Company standards.

10. Services Provided by the Company

10.1 High-Level Financing Access. The Company provides exclusive lender partnerships, monthly payment options as low as $200–$600+ depending on client credit, high approval rates, and financing options not available to most companies.

10.2 Sales Infrastructure. The Company provides access to a high-converting sales team, enrollment specialists, and webinar optimization experts.

10.3 Marketing Support. The Company provides access to top advertising channels, assistance verifying and applying ad spend, offer positioning, and webinar strategy.

10.4 Program Optimization. After successful completion of the Trial Webinar and approval as a full Partner, the Company may introduce optional program optimization opportunities designed to support stronger client outcomes, higher completion rates, and long-term lender confidence. As part of this support, Partners may be invited to transition their program delivery to Kajabi or a similar platform to enable deeper insight into client engagement, participation, and completion. This transition is intended to help Partners: (a) better understand how clients are progressing through their program; (b) identify early signs of disengagement; (c) improve overall client experience and satisfaction; and (d) strengthen consistency in client participation and repayment behavior.

10.5 In-House Media Buying Support. The Company provides each Partner with access to its in-house media buying team to support and scale advertising efforts around webinars and financed offers. The Company covers all advertising costs upfront. Any advertising spend associated with Partner campaigns is transparently tracked and recouped from gross revenue prior to profit-sharing in accordance with Section 4.

11. Payout Timelines and Lender Variability

11.1 Multiple Lenders. The Company works with multiple lenders. Payout timelines vary: some lenders pay in three to five (3–5) business days; some pay within two (2) weeks; and some pay on the loan origination date, typically four to five (4–5) days after approval.

11.2 Origination Date. Clients are informed of their loan origination date because they personally accept it during the financing process.

11.3 Company Obligations. The Company will: (a) notify the Partner of each client's loan origination date; (b) provide reports with tracking and payout schedules; and (c) pay Partner commissions within two (2) business days after receiving funds from the lender.

12. Confidentiality and Non-Disclosure

12.1 Confidential Information. During the course of this partnership, the Partner may access confidential materials including but not limited to: sales processes, client lists, training systems, webinar scripts, marketing strategies, operations, pricing structures, financing partner details, and proprietary business methods.

12.2 Obligations. The Partner agrees: (a) not to disclose, replicate, reverse-engineer, or distribute any confidential materials to any third party; (b) not to use Company information for personal or competing business activities; and (c) to return or destroy all confidential materials upon termination of this Agreement.

12.3 Survival. The confidentiality obligations under this Section 12 shall survive the termination of this Agreement indefinitely.

13. Non-Solicitation and Non-Circumvention

13.1 Non-Solicitation. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of two (2) years following termination, the Partner shall not, directly or indirectly: (a) solicit, recruit, hire, or engage any employee, contractor, enrollment specialist, webinar host, media buyer, or agent of the Company; (b) solicit, contact, or attempt to do business directly with any financing partner, lender, or funding source introduced through the Company; or (c) solicit or recruit other Company Partners for competing ventures.

13.2 Non-Circumvention. The Partner shall not circumvent, avoid, bypass, or attempt to circumvent this Agreement to: (a) work directly with any Company financing partner without the Company's involvement; (b) replicate the Company's business model using information gained through this partnership; or (c) deprive the Company of any business opportunity, relationship, or revenue stream.

13.3 Liquidated Damages. Any violation of this Section 13 shall result in immediate liquidated damages of Two Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars ($250,000.00) per occurrence, which the Parties agree is a reasonable estimate of the damages the Company would suffer and not a penalty.

14. Non-Disparagement

14.1 Non-Disparagement Obligation. The Partner agrees that they shall not, at any time during or after the term of this Agreement, make any disparaging, defamatory, derogatory, negative, or damaging statements, comments, or remarks, whether written, oral, electronic, or otherwise, about the Company, its owners, officers, directors, managers, members, employees, contractors, agents, webinar hosts, financing partners, clients, products, services, business practices, or reputation.

14.2 Scope. This non-disparagement obligation extends to, but is not limited to, statements made on social media platforms, websites, forums, review sites, in private conversations, in public settings, to media outlets, or to any third party.

14.3 Liquidated Damages. Any violation of this Section 14 shall result in liquidated damages of One Hundred Thousand Dollars ($100,000.00) per infringement or occurrence. The Parties acknowledge and agree that the harm caused by disparagement is difficult to quantify, and that this amount represents a reasonable and good-faith estimate of the damages the Company would suffer and is not intended as a penalty.

14.4 Mutual Non-Disparagement. The Company likewise agrees not to make disparaging statements about the Partner. This mutual obligation does not apply to truthful statements made in connection with legal proceedings, regulatory inquiries, or as otherwise required by law.

15. Intellectual Property

15.1 Company Ownership. All intellectual property belonging to the Company, including but not limited to sales processes, scripts, training materials, webinar content, branding, enrollment systems, and marketing assets, shall remain the sole and exclusive property of the Company.

15.2 Partner Ownership. The Partner retains full ownership of their coaching, consulting, or training offer, curriculum, course content, and any intellectual property that existed prior to entering this Agreement.

15.3 Joint Work Product. Any webinar recordings, promotional materials, or sales assets created jointly during the partnership shall be co-owned by both Parties. Neither Party may use joint work product for purposes that compete with or harm the other Party without prior written consent.

16. Indemnification

16.1 Indemnification by Partner. The Partner shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company, its officers, directors, managers, members, employees, agents, and affiliates from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorney's fees) arising out of or relating to: (a) the Partner's breach of this Agreement; (b) the Partner's negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud; (c) the Partner's failure to fulfill their offer or deliver services to clients; (d) any client complaint, refund demand, or legal action arising from the Partner's program or services; or (e) any third-party claim arising from the Partner's performance or failure to perform under this Agreement.

16.2 Indemnification by Company. The Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Partner from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorney's fees) arising out of or relating to the Company's breach of this Agreement or the Company's gross negligence or willful misconduct.

17. Dispute Resolution

17.1 Arbitration. Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or relating to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, shall be resolved exclusively through binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association ("AAA") in accordance with its Commercial Arbitration Rules. Arbitration shall be the first and mandatory form of dispute resolution before any Party may seek judicial relief, except as provided in Section 17.4.

17.2 Location of Arbitration. All arbitration proceedings shall take place in Miami-Dade County, Florida.

17.3 Arbitration Award. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding on both Parties. Judgment upon the award rendered by the arbitrator may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

17.4 Injunctive Relief. Notwithstanding the foregoing, either Party may seek temporary or preliminary injunctive relief from a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm pending the outcome of arbitration, including but not limited to enforcement of the confidentiality, non-solicitation, non-circumvention, and non-disparagement provisions of this Agreement.

18. Attorney's Fees and Costs

18.1 Prevailing Party. In the event that any legal action, arbitration, or proceeding is commenced to enforce the terms of this Agreement or to resolve any dispute arising hereunder, the non-prevailing Party shall pay all reasonable attorney's fees, costs, and expenses incurred by the prevailing Party.

18.2 Breach. If either Party is required to engage legal counsel or initiate any legal proceeding as a result of a breach or alleged breach by the other Party, the breaching Party shall be responsible for and shall reimburse the non-breaching Party for all reasonable attorney's fees, legal costs, and expenses incurred, regardless of whether formal proceedings are initiated.

19. Limitation of Liability

19.1 Except for breaches of Sections 12, 13, and 14 of this Agreement (Confidentiality, Non-Solicitation, and Non-Disparagement), neither Party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, incidental, consequential, special, or punitive damages, regardless of the form of action, whether in contract, tort, or otherwise, even if such Party has been advised of the possibility of such damages.

20. Independent Contractor Status

The Partner is an independent contractor and not an employee of the Company. This Agreement does not create a joint venture, employment, or agency relationship between the Parties. Neither Party has the authority to bind the other or to incur obligations on behalf of the other. The Partner shall be solely responsible for all taxes, insurance, and other obligations arising from payments received under this Agreement.

21. Representations and Warranties

Each Party represents and warrants to the other that: (a) it has full power and authority to enter into this Agreement; (b) the execution and performance of this Agreement does not conflict with any other agreement or obligation; (c) this Agreement constitutes a valid and binding obligation enforceable in accordance with its terms; and (d) all information provided in connection with this Agreement is true, accurate, and complete.

The Partner additionally represents and warrants that: (a) they possess a legitimate coaching, consulting, training, or service offer with genuine value to clients; (b) they will deliver all services promised to clients enrolled through Company-facilitated webinars; (c) they are not subject to any restrictive covenant or agreement that would prevent them from performing under this Agreement; and (d) they will comply with all applicable laws and regulations in the performance of their duties.

22. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

22.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Florida, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

22.2 Jurisdiction. Subject to the arbitration provisions of Section 17, the Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the state and federal courts located in Miami-Dade County, Florida for any action or proceeding arising out of or relating to this Agreement.

23. General Provisions

23.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether written or oral.

23.2 Amendments. This Agreement may not be amended, modified, or supplemented except by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

23.3 Waiver. No waiver of any provision of this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party. No failure or delay in exercising any right shall operate as a waiver thereof.

23.4 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable while preserving the Parties' original intent.

23.5 Assignment. The Partner may not assign or transfer this Agreement or any rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the Company. The Company may assign this Agreement freely to any successor, affiliate, or assignee.

23.6 Notices. All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when delivered personally, sent by certified mail (return receipt requested), or sent by nationally recognized overnight courier to the addresses set forth above or to such other address as a Party may designate in writing.

23.7 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic or digital signatures shall be deemed original signatures for all purposes.

23.8 Force Majeure. Neither Party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performing its obligations under this Agreement to the extent such failure or delay is caused by circumstances beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to acts of God, war, terrorism, pandemic, natural disaster, internet outage, or government action.

23.9 Third-Party Beneficiaries. This Agreement is intended solely for the benefit of the Parties and their respective permitted successors and assigns. Nothing herein shall confer any rights or remedies upon any third party.

23.10 Headings. The section headings in this Agreement are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

23.11 Construction. This Agreement shall be construed without regard to any presumption or rule requiring construction against the Party causing this Agreement to be drafted.
